## Scientific boundary

This was a synthetic infrastructure probe, not a fresh heldout experiment. It
did not inspect, embed, or reuse any P1v3 heldout outcome. It does not add
evidence for policy actuation, result-to-credit-to-`ΔW` learning, Agent A to B
transfer, topology rewiring, or consolidation.

The frontier question `Q-p1v4-server-replay-compatible-judge-seam` remains open
until a new preregistered unseen heldout cut uses this seam and itself receives
`replay_status=verified`. Closing it from the synthetic probe would overstate
the evidence.

## Next fresh-heldout protocol

1. Freeze the judge bytes and SHA before prediction registration.
2. Freeze the evidence/budget schema and the unseen-cut budget before opening
   the heldout sidecar.
3. Register the prediction with the frozen judge SHA.
4. Measure the new cut once; do not open or reuse P1v3 heldout outcomes.
5. Build one self-contained bundle from the sealed evidence and budget; never
   copy the client-reported metric into it.
6. Deploy the exact judge and bundle bytes into a persistent
   LakatoTree-allowed execution root.
7. Submit the independently computed value and require all of:
   `script_sha_server_verified=true`, `replay_status=verified`, and
   `measurement_grade=server_regenerated`.

The current `/tmp` deployment was intentionally limited to the synthetic live
probe. The fresh experiment must use a persistent allowed root so later audits
can rerun it after service restarts.
